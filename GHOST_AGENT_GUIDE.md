## 🧠 How Ghost Analyzes Your Artifacts

### The Analysis Process

**Step 1: Image Detection**
```
You upload a photo → Ghost detects it
Ghost thinks (fading text effect) → "Processing heritage insights..."
```

**Step 2: Smart Questions**
Ghost asks contextual questions:
- "What's the story behind this object?"
- "Where is this tradition practiced?"
- "Who taught you this skill?"
- "Why is this important to your family?"

**Step 3: Temporal Analysis**
Ghost evaluates:
- **Current Relevance**: Why does this matter RIGHT NOW in 2026?
- **Future Importance**: Why will this matter in 50-100 years?
- **Preservation Urgency**: How rare is this? How quickly is it disappearing?

**Step 4: Time Capsule Note**
Ghost generates a poetic statement:
> "In a world where every craft is mechanized, the hand-woven Navajo blanket becomes a bridge between generations—a physical proof that beauty requires patience, intention, and soul."

*Deep thinking...* 🧠

**Analysis**:
- **Current Relevance**: Languages encode unique ways of understanding the world—yours might have words for concepts no other language captures
- **Future Importance**: In 100 years, linguists will study this as evidence of human cognitive diversity
- **Preservation Urgency**: 9/10 - CRITICAL - Each speaker is irreplaceable
- **Time Capsule Note**: 'Your language holds thoughts that have never been thought in any other tongue. When it's spoken, history whispers through the present.'"

## 🎯 Preservation Urgency Scale

**10/10 - CRITICAL**
- Only a handful of practitioners remain
- Language with <50 speakers
- Endangered craft facing extinction

**7-9/10 - HIGH**
- Rapidly decreasing practitioners
- Cultural practice losing younger generation interest
- Traditional knowledge not being passed on

**4-6/10 - MODERATE**
- Stable but slowly declining
- Some revitalization efforts underway
- Documentation exists but incomplete

**1-3/10 - STABLE**
- Well-documented and practiced
- Strong community interest
- Actively being taught to new generations
